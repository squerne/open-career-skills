# Cut onboarding from six weeks to nine days

**Tags:** process redesign, training, stakeholder management, documentation

## Situation
Example Corp's support team was scaling from 12 to 30 agents in one quarter, but new agents took six weeks to reach full ticket load. The VP flagged onboarding as the top blocker for the expansion.

## Task
I owned redesigning the onboarding program end to end, with a hard deadline of the next hiring cohort (five weeks out).

## Action
I shadowed the three most recent hires to find where time actually went: 60% of the six weeks was waiting for ad-hoc shadowing slots. I wrote a self-serve training playbook covering the 20 most common ticket types, recorded walkthroughs for the internal tools, and replaced open-ended shadowing with three structured half-day rotations. I negotiated with two team leads to guarantee rotation slots for every cohort.

## Result
The next cohort reached full ticket load in nine days. The playbook became the team standard and was adopted by the Berlin office the following quarter. Agent 90-day retention went from 78% to 91% over the next two cohorts.
